Singapore: The City-State Explained

Country, City, or Something Else?

The Big Question

It's a common point of confusion. Singapore is a tiny dot on the world map, yet it's a major global player. It's 100% urban, so it feels like a city. But it has its own government, currency, and military, so it acts like a country. So, what is it?

The Simple Answer: It's a City-State

The most accurate term for Singapore is a **city-state**. This is a special category for a place that is, at the same time, both a sovereign country and a single city. Singapore is one of only three in the world today, alongside Monaco and Vatican City.

Singapore is a fully independent and sovereign nation. It was not a city that "belonged" to a larger country; it became an independent country in 1965. It has all the key components of a modern state.

A Brief Journey to City-Statehood

Singapore's unique status is a result of its specific history. It wasn't always independent.

1819: British Trading Post

Sir Stamford Raffles establishes a trading post, marking the start of modern Singapore.

1959: Self-Governance

Singapore achieves self-governance from the British Empire, but is not yet fully independent.

1963: Merger with Malaysia

Singapore briefly joins the Federation of Malaysia, becoming a state within the new country.

1965: Full Independence

After political and economic disagreements, Singapore separates from Malaysia and becomes a fully sovereign and independent nation. The modern city-state is born.

Putting It in Perspective

Comparing Singapore to other places highlights its unique nature. It has the land area of a city but the density of a major metropolis, all while being a sovereign nation.

Comparison: Total Area (sq km)

Singapore's total land area is very small, comparable to a single large city like New York City and much larger than the micro-state of Monaco.

Comparison: Population Density (people / sq km)

While Monaco is the most crowded, Singapore's population density is among the highest in the world, far exceeding most other countries and cities.

Conclusion: A Successful Modern City-State

So, is Singapore a country or a city? The answer is **both**.

It is a **country** because it is a fully sovereign, independent nation.
It is a **city** because it is a single, unified urban area.
This makes it a perfect modern example of a **city-state**.
